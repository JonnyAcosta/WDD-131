Famous Historical Sites in Mexico

Chichen Itza - A major Mayan city and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.one of the most famous and fascinating archaeological sites in Mexico and the world. The name Chichén Itzá means “at the mouth of the well of the Itzá” in the Maya language, referring to a nearby cenote (natural sinkhole) that was important for water and possibly for rituals.

Teotihuacan - Known for its massive pyramids, it was once one of the largest cities in the world. Is a massive ancient city in central Mexico, located near today’s Mexico City. Is famous for its massive structures aligned with the stars and sun

Angel of Independence - is one of the most iconic monuments in Mexico City and a powerful symbol of Mexican national pride and independence. It’s a monument built to commemorate the centennial of Mexico’s War of Independence, which began in 1810.

Estadio Azteca - One of the most famous football stadiums in the world, hosting two World Cups. Is a massive sports stadium located in Mexico City. It’s mostly used for soccer (fútbol) but has also hosted concerts, political events, and even religious gatherings. It’s famous for being one of the most historic and recognized stadiums in the world. Is is a symbol of Mexican pride in sports and culture. It is also one of the biggest stadium in the world by having a capacity of over 100,000.
